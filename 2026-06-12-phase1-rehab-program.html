01

Design Logic

Three upstream problems drive this program, in order of influence:

1. Left glute medius weakness (107N vs 130N right, ~18% deficit) is the root cause of lateral patellar tracking and the knee problem. Glute activation before every knee loading session is non-negotiable.

2. Right medial epicondylitis — vascularization and eccentric tendon loading track. Left elbow maintenance on the same protocol, lighter load.

3. First MTP joint sensitivity (both feet, right worse) — systematic foot strengthening with protected progression toward rebounder and hill running.

Session invariant: Glute activation (Block 1) must precede knee loading within 20–30 minutes. This is the only non-negotiable sequence constraint. Everything else can float.

Day A — Lower Body Emphasis

~70 minutes

Block 1 — Mandatory Glute Activation

Must precede knee loading. If short on time — skip Block 2, not Block 1.

Exercise Sets × Reps Equipment Key Cues
Mini-band Clamshell 3 × 15 LEFT / 3 × 10 RIGHT Red mini loop band Tempo 2-1-2. Hand on left hip bone — pelvis must not rotate. Movement from hip only, not TFL.
Side-lying Hip Abduction (no band) 2 × 20 LEFT Mat Leg slightly behind body, toes slightly down. Recruits glute medius, not TFL.
Single-leg Glute Bridge 3 × 12 LEFT / 2 × 12 RIGHT Mat Drive through heel. Hold 2 sec at peak. Can't feel left glute? Don't proceed to knee loading.

Block 2 — Knee Loading (glute activation confirmed)

Exercise Sets × Reps / Duration Equipment Key Cues
Wall Sit Isometric 3 × 30–45 sec at 60° knee flexion — Actively push left knee outward. Pain 0–2/10 OK; 3+/10 shorten duration, not depth. Alternative: Spanish squat — loop a resistance band around a fixed point at knee height, stand facing it with band behind both knees, sit back into a squat keeping shins vertical. Same VMO stimulus, different feel. Horse stance (wide isometric squat, toes out) is a reasonable hip-opener drill but does not substitute for this — it loads adductors and quads, not the glute medius, and the depth increases patellofemoral stress. Revisit horse stance in Phase 2 when left knee is settled.
Terminal Knee Extension (TKE) 3 × 15 each leg Ankle-cuff tubing, low anchor Full extension, feel VMO at terminal range. Control the return.
Lateral Step-Down 3 × 10 each leg Yoga block or bottom stair Stand sideways, lower opposite foot under control. Left knee tracks over 2nd toe. Valgus = stop, regardless of pain.
Hip Thrust Wks 1–2: 3 × 15 BW · Wks 3–4: 4 × 12 @ 25 lb dumbbells Bench Drive to full hip extension, 1-sec glute squeeze at top. Actively push left knee outward throughout. Highest-EMG glute exercise.
Romanian Deadlift (RDL) Wks 1–2: 3 × 10 light · Wks 3–4: 3 × 10 moderate Dumbbells or barbell Hip hinge. Glutes drive lockout — not low back extension. Zero knee pain expected.

Block 3 — Core

Exercise Sets × Reps / Duration Equipment Key Cues
Dead Bug 3 × 6 each side Mat Neutral spine. Exhale fully as limb extends. 3s extension, 2s hold, 3s return. Head flat, chin neutral. Right arm: no gripping weight while medial epicondylitis active.
Pallof Press 3 × 8–10 each side Mini loop band, door anchor Knees soft, hip width. Press from sternum, hold 2s, return. No right elbow lock-out — stop just short. Anti-rotation work, not a load exercise.
Roman Chair Hyperextension 3 × 12 Roman chair Hold at current load — no progression until dead bug and pallof press are established.

Block 4 — Cardio

Tunturi E455 upright bike or recumbent bike — 20 minutes, Zone 2 / conversational pace. Seat height: knee at ~25–30° at bottom of stroke.

Day B — Upper Body + Elbow Rehab

~55 minutes

Block 1 — Elbow Rehab — Both Arms, One Protocol

Same exercises for both arms. Right arm uses load it tolerates per pain rules. Left arm uses lighter resistance / shorter duration. No separate tracking.

Exercise Sets × Reps Equipment Right Arm Left Arm
Wrist Roller 2 × full cycle each direction Forearm/wrist roller (the handle with a rope attached to a weight peg) No added plates Wks 1–2 — just the roller itself. Hold the handle in front of you with arms extended, then rotate the wrists to wind the rope up, then unwind it back down. One full wind-up and wind-down = one cycle. Palmar flexion emphasis. Pain 0–3/10. Same but lighter / slower. Monitor for lateral elbow sensation — drop if present.
Eccentric Wrist Curl 3 × 10 5 lb dumbbell Sit with forearm resting on thigh, palm up, 5 lb dumbbell in the working hand. Use the other hand to lift the wrist into the curled position (concentric handled by the helper hand — do not curl with the working arm). Release the helper hand. Lower the weight slowly over 4 counts with the working arm only. That slow lowering is the entire point — the eccentric phase is where the tendon remodelling happens. Wrist stays between neutral and slight palmar flex — do not extend past neutral. Same exercise, lighter effort. 3 lb or use just the bar of the dumbbell if 5 lb is too much.
Finger Spring Trainer 2 × 15 per finger Individual-finger spring trainer Full protocol — ring and little finger emphasis. Maintenance volume: 2 × 10 per finger.
Foam Ball Grip 2 × 20 squeezes Teal or yellow ball Start teal; if 3+/10 use yellow. Full squeeze, 1-sec hold, slow release. Yellow ball. 2 × 15.

Block 2 — Bench + Pull + Shoulders

Exercise Sets × Reps Equipment Key Cues
Light Bench Press 3 × 12–15, RPE 5–6/10 Barbell, bench Vascularization stimulus, not strength. Pain 0–2/10 medial elbow OK; 3+/10 reduce load.
Ring Rows (low angle) 3 × 8–10 Gymnastics rings (hung at roughly waist height) Set the rings low — roughly waist height. Grip the rings, walk your feet forward and lean back until your body is at about 30–40° from the floor (so your body is mostly upright, not horizontal). Arms are straight. Pull yourself toward the rings by bending the elbows, keeping them tucked. Lower back to straight arms under control. The more you walk your feet forward (the more horizontal your body), the harder it gets — start with body mostly upright. This is a rowing movement, not a pull-up. The elbow angle and load are much more forgiving on the medial epicondyle than any vertical pulling. Advance the angle (walk feet further out) as the right elbow tolerates.
Dumbbell Overhead Press 3 × 10 Dumbbells Light to moderate. Stop if elbow pain appears.
Band Pull-Apart (overhand) 3 × 15 Mini loop band Posterior cuff maintenance.

Block 3 — Core

Exercise Sets × Reps / Duration Equipment Key Cues
Dead Bug 3 × 6 each side Mat Same as Day A. Daily frequency appropriate — not recovery-limited.
Swiss Ball Rollout or Plank 3 × 30–45 sec Swiss ball or floor Plank: if right MTP sensitive, use knees-down variant. Maintain lumbar neutral throughout.

Daily — Elbow Isometric Protocol

Every day including rest days. Seated, TV-friendly. 5–7 minutes.
Equipment: Two foam grip balls (yellow = soft, teal = firm), individual-finger spring trainer.
Position: Seated, elbow at ~90°, forearm resting on thigh or armrest.
Both arms — same protocol. Adjust load per arm (right heavier as tolerated; left lighter).

Step 1 — Wrist Isometric Holds (3 min per arm)

Forearm resting on thigh.

Hold Reps / Duration Notes
Palm UP — palmar flexion isometric Loose fist, press gently into thigh. Hold 10 sec × 5. Rest 5 sec between. Loads common flexor origin (medial epicondyle). Primary hold for golfer's elbow.
Palm DOWN — wrist extension isometric Forearm on thigh, palm down. Press back of hand gently up against light resistance. Hold 10 sec × 5. Loads common extensor origin (lateral epicondyle). Primary hold for tennis elbow. Both conditions present in both arms to varying degrees.
Grip ball isometric Yellow ball (start); teal when yellow is easy. Squeeze firm, hold 10 sec, release fully × 5. Right arm: teal if 0–2/10 pain. Left arm: yellow.
Finger spring trainer 10 slow presses per finger, hold 2 sec at peak. Ring and little finger emphasis. Right arm: full set. Left arm: 8 reps per finger.

Step 2 — Forearm Stretches (1–2 min per arm)

Stretch Hold Notes
Wrist flexor stretch (palm up) Extend arm, palm up, pull fingers back gently with other hand. 30 sec × 2. Right arm primary — addresses medial epicondyle.
Wrist extensor stretch (palm down) Extend arm, palm down, pull hand gently downward. 30 sec × 2. Left arm primary — addresses lateral epicondyle. Both arms benefit.

On training days after bench press or ring rows: right arm may be more sensitive — reduce grip ball intensity that evening.

07

Foot Strengthening + First MTP Management

Can be paired with daily elbow protocol. Mostly seated or standing — TV and kitchen-friendly.

Exercise Sets × Reps Notes
Toe Yoga (big toe isolation) 3 × 10 alternations per foot Lift big toe only; then press big toe, lift lesser toes. Foundational intrinsic foot work.
Short Foot / Foot Doming 3 × 10 holds per foot Draw ball of foot toward heel without curling toes. Practice during wobble board standing.
Towel Scrunches 2 × 30 sec per foot Toe flexion to scrunch towel. Loads flexors without MTP hyperextension.
Toe Resistance (mini band) 3 × 15 per foot (extra set right) Band around toes, fixed at ball of foot. Flex toes against resistance.
Heel-only Calf Raise (from step) 3 × 12–15 each leg Only heel on step edge — ball of foot and toes hang free. No toe-off MTP stress. Add weight when easy.

Phase 1 Progressions

Rebounder: Health bounce — two feet, low amplitude, feet flat on mat, 10 min max. Stop if any MTP pain. No toe-off intensity, no single-leg work.

Hill running: Flat and mild terrain only. Shoes with 6–8 mm+ heel drop. No sustained uphill in Phase 1.

Doc flag: First MTP joint requires clinical assessment before Phase 2 rebounder and hill running. Kinec is holding Phase 2 progressions on these two modalities pending Doc's input.

Non-negotiable anchor: Glute activation (Block 1) must precede knee loading within 20–30 minutes.

Can be done at any time of day

  • Single-leg RDL (bodyweight) — kitchen, garden, anytime standing
  • Wobble board balance — evening news, anywhere standing
  • Toe yoga + short foot + towel scrunches — seated anywhere
  • Daily elbow isometric protocol — TV every evening

  • Step-down / step-up (loaded)
  • Goblet squat
  • Loaded RDL
  • Ring rows
  • Bench press
  • Wall sit
  • Pallof press

Progression Criteria

Peter does not experience morning-after ache. All gates use intra-session and during-activity pain as the primary signal.

Pain Scale

Level Response
0–2/10 Continue and progress normally
3/10 Complete set, do not add load this session
4/10 Stop that exercise, substitute or skip
5+/10 Stop session; flag to Doc if it recurs

Phase 1 → Phase 2 Gates (all must be true)

  • Left knee 0–2/10 on lateral step-down for 3 consecutive sessions, zero valgus
  • Wall sit 45 sec pain-free
  • Left glute firing equal to or before right in single-leg bridge
  • Right elbow bench press 0–2/10 for 4 consecutive sessions
  • Rebounder health bounce (10 min) produces 0/10 MTP pain
  • Hip thrust 3 × 15 BW with 1-sec hold, no left knee valgus

Right Elbow Road Map

Stage 1 Phase 1, Wks 1–4 Daily isometrics + towel eccentric + wrist roller BW + ring rows low angle
Stage 2 Phase 2, Wks 5–8 Eccentric wrist curl progresses to 8 lb dumbbell; wrist roller with a small added plate (1.25 kg); ring rows with feet walked further forward so body is closer to horizontal (harder); farmer's carry simulation — stand still holding 15 lb dumbbells at your sides for 20–30 seconds, no walking yet, to build grip and elbow tolerance before the full carry
Stage 3 Phase 3, Wks 9–12 Bent-arm supported hang; farmer's carry @ 15 lb; ring rows at full horizontal
Stage 4 Phase 4+ Passive hang; eccentric-only pull-ups; farmer's carry progression
No change after 6 weeks of eccentric loading → flag to Doc (possible ulnar nerve or capsular involvement).
12

Red Flags — Stop and Flag to Doc

  • Sharp / hot / stabbing knee pain during any exercise
  • New clicking, locking, or giving-way in either knee
  • Right elbow pain radiating toward ring/little finger, or tingling/numbness in forearm or hand
  • Any elbow pain above 5/10 not reducing within 10 min of stopping
  • First MTP pain not resolving within 24 hours, or any joint swelling
  • Instability or giving-way at knee or ankle
  • Pain at rest in any affected joint (not reported — flag immediately if it appears)
